Malik Wilson

Engineer

I proudly served in the United States Navy, where I developed a strong foundation in operational readiness, technical expertise, and leadership under pressure. During my time in the service, I was entrusted with critical responsibilities and participated in various high-stakes inspections, including INSURV (Board of Inspection and Survey), light-offs, and main space fire drills. These experiences sharpened my attention to detail, safety discipline, and ability to perform in high-stress environments.

I earned key qualifications in engineering spaces, including Main Space Monitor, Auxiliary Space Rover, and Auxiliary Space Operator. These roles demanded thorough technical knowledge of shipboard mechanical and electrical systems, as well as the ability to troubleshoot under real-world operational conditions. Achieving these qualifications required not only technical skill but also the discipline to maintain systems vital to mission success.

I have completed one overseas deployment, an experience that broadened my perspective, reinforced my adaptability, and strengthened my ability to work as part of a diverse and highly capable team. My deployment tested and refined both my technical and interpersonal skills, particularly in time-sensitive and mission-critical operations. I gained confidence working independently and as part of a larger command structure, contributing to ship readiness and crew safety.
